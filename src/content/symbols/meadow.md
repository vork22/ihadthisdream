The meadow is open ground where ease becomes possible. The Psalmist's green pastures are meadows. Jungian analysis reads meadow-dreams as the psyche's report of its own rest — or its longing for rest. A meadow crossed is often an easy passage between harder terrains. Notice the flowers, the animals, and whether you lie down.
