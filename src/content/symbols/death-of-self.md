Dreams of one's own death are, with rare exception, not literal. Jungian analysis treats them as images of a phase of the self completing — a role, identity, relationship, or worldview that is dying to make room for what is forming. Alchemical tradition calls this stage the nigredo, the blackening, the necessary death before the work can continue. Shamanic initiations across cultures involve a dream or vision of the initiate's death and reassembly. The mode of dream-death matters: peaceful death often marks a conscious release; violent death, a transition happening against the ego's will; waking before the moment of death, a threshold not yet crossed. Notice who is with you when you die in the dream, and what you are released from.
