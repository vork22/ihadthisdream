Flying dreams are among the most cherished across cultures — shamanic traditions treat them as literal soul-flight, evidence of the spirit's capacity to leave the body and move freely. Jungian analysis reads flying-dreams more cautiously: flight can express genuine transcendence, or it can be a compensatory escape from embodied life. The quality of the flight matters. Easy, buoyant flight is usually a good omen — the psyche reporting a real expansion of capacity. Flight that you must effortfully sustain, or that keeps dragging you down toward power lines and treetops, often suggests aspirations outpacing the self's current grounding. Notice what you fly over, and whether you fly alone.
