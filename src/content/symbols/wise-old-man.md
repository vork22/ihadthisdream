The Wise Old Man is Jung's archetype of meaning — an inner figure who arrives in dreams bearing counsel the ego has not yet found on its own. He appears across mythology as Merlin, Gandalf, the Hermit of the tarot, the Taoist sage on the mountain. In dreams he often comes at inflection points: the threshold of a vocation, the beginning of a grief, the moment before a decision. His counsel is rarely practical and often obscure; the dream-encounter itself is often the point, more than any message delivered. Notice whether he speaks, hands you an object, or simply waits for you to ask. The archetype can be exhausting in shadow form (the know-it-all, the lecturer); in its integrated form it is deeply orienting.
