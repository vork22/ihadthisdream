The spider is one of the most polyvalent dream-symbols on record. The
register depends partly on the dreamer's cultural context. See the
expanded dream-entry [Spiders](/dreams/spiders) for the full treatment.

## The Jungian reading

Jung treats the spider in two registers (CW 5): the *weaver* (slow
patient creation) and the *terrible mother* (devouring, web-binding).
Both are valid; both appear in dreams.

## Cross-cultural readings

Spider Woman in Pueblo, Diné, and Hopi traditions is a creator-weaver
of profound cultural weight. Anansi in West African and Caribbean
traditions is creator-trickster.

## If the dream changes

- **From threat to weaver.** The dreamer has begun to see the creation
  rather than only the threat.
- **From a single spider to many.** An accumulating entanglement.
- **From web to no web.** Material has been addressed.
