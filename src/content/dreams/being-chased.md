Jung would call the pursuer a shadow figure — an unacknowledged part of the self. The dream's urgency is proportional to the degree of avoidance in waking life. In the Ojibwe and Lakota traditions, being chased by an animal may also mark the arrival of a spirit ally demanding recognition.
