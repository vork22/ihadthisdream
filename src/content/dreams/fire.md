Fire is the only element that changes everything it touches. It cooks
food, smelts metal, lights the room, and ends the building. Unsurprisingly,
it is one of the most polyvalent dream symbols on record. The
*Atharvaveda* addresses Agni — fire personified — as both household god
and inner sacrifice. Heraclitus called the cosmos "an ever-living fire."
The Christian Pentecost is fire descending in the form of tongues. Sufi
poetry, from Rumi to Hafiz to Ibn al-Farid, is fire shot through.

When fire appears in your dream, ask first what the fire is *doing*.

## The Jungian reading

Jung treated fire as the energetic substrate of the unconscious in its
*transformative* aspect (CW 5; CW 12 on alchemy). Where water-symbols
image the unconscious as medium, fire-symbols image it as agent. In
*Symbols of Transformation* he gathers fire-dreams clustering around
major life changes: house-fires before divorces, wildfires before
resignations, candles being lit at the start of analytic work. The
friendliness or violence of the dream-fire tracks the conscious self's
relation to the change.

## The Freudian reading

Freud's reading is narrower: certain childhood fire-dreams as bedwetting
anxiety, certain adult fire-dreams as suppressed sexual content. Most
useful when the dream-fire arrives in an explicitly erotic setting.

## Vedic, Greek, Sufi readings

Agni is the central god of the *Atharvaveda* — household hearth,
cremation flame, the inner *jathar-agni* (digestive fire). To dream of
Agni clearly is in Vedic dream-classification an unambiguously auspicious
sign. The Greek inheritance is double: Hephaestus's productive forge-fire
and Prometheus's transgressive stolen fire. The Christian Pentecost (Acts
2) makes fire the literal form of the descending Spirit. Sufi mystical
poetry treats fire overwhelmingly as a symbol of erotic-mystical longing
— the moth into the candle.

## Why this dream recurs

Recurrent fire-dreams cluster in periods of major personal change — career,
relationship, identity, recovery — that the conscious self has been
trying to slow down. The recurrence usually softens once the change is
allowed.

## If the dream changes

- **From wildfire to hearth.** The transformation has been brought into
  containment.
- **From burning house to ash.** The change has completed; the next
  dream-cycle is the rebuild.
- **From feared fire to walked-into fire.** The dreamer has accepted the
  initiation.

## When to take it seriously

Fire-dreams in survivors of physical fire trauma need trauma-informed
care, not dream-dictionary lookups.
