One of the most universally reported dreams. Freud read falling as a symbol of yielding to temptation or moral 'descent.' Jung saw it as the ego's confrontation with the unconscious — a necessary sinking before renewal. Many Indigenous dream traditions view falling as a sign of ungroundedness, a call to return to the body and the land.
